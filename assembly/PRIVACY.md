# Privacy Notice

*Last updated: 14 July 2026*

## What this is about

The Green Software Foundation (GSF) is running an AI-assisted consensus exercise, the SCI for OpenTelemetry Assembly. Around 40 participants work over roughly 10 to 12 weeks, entirely by email, to agree how the Software Carbon Intensity (SCI) specification should be represented in OpenTelemetry's semantic conventions. This notice explains how we handle your personal data during that process.

The short version: we collect your email and your responses, we separate your identity from your responses before any AI processing, and we publish agreed outcomes and anonymised synthesis rather than your individual responses. How your contributions are licensed and used in the deliverable is covered separately in the participation terms.

## What we collect

- **Your name and email address**, to send you questions, syntheses and updates, and to run the process.
- **Your responses**, meaning the thoughts and positions you share in each round.
- **Optional details you choose to provide**, such as your organisation and LinkedIn if you opt into the participant directory.

We do not collect demographic information, IP addresses, or any other personal data beyond what you provide.

## How we process your responses

In every phase, your contributions are seen only by GSF staff, the facilitator and the AI, which receives them with your identity removed. Other participants never see your responses or your name. What you receive back is synthesis and candidate positions representing the group's views, not anyone's individual words.

- **Discover.** You send open responses by email; identity is separated from content before processing.
- **Deliberate and Decide.** You react to AI-synthesised candidate positions by rating, commenting, and finally endorsing, consenting or objecting. Your reactions feed the next synthesis and are not shown to other participants.

Your name and email are stored separately from your response content and are used to communicate with you, not combined with your responses in any system where AI processing occurs.

## About the AI processing

We use Anthropic's Claude to synthesise consensus from responses.

- Claude works on anonymised content, without names or email addresses.
- Consensus statements and synthesis may quote or paraphrase what participants said, but are never attributed to an individual.
- We use Anthropic's standard commercial API. Under their terms they do not train on API inputs, though standard processing and logging may occur.

## Transparency and how it is kept private

Consensus work needs to be trustworthy, and the way we make it verifiable is different from publishing everyone's raw input.

- **Your individual responses are kept private.** We do not publish them, either with your name or as raw anonymised text.
- **What is published is the outcome.** The agreed consensus text and anonymised synthesis are shared with GSF membership and the OpenTelemetry community, and the drafted semantic conventions that result are submitted to the OpenTelemetry project as open source. These are the group's agreed outputs, not your personal data.
- **Verifiability without exposure.** Instead of publishing raw responses, we keep a cryptographic record (proofs over responses, ratings and votes) and the synthesis history. This lets the process be audited end to end without making individual contributions public. It also means participants whose organisations restrict public positions can take part safely.
- **Distinctive detail.** Where synthesis is shared, direct identifiers are removed, but if you include highly distinctive phrasing or specific personal details, a response could in theory be recognised. Please bear this in mind.

## Your rights

- **Opt out at any time.** Reply to any Assembly email with "unsubscribe" and we will remove you from all future communications.
- **Request deletion.** Email us to have your personal information deleted. Because our audit record uses cryptographic proofs rather than stored raw content, removing your identifiers and response content does not require us to expose anything to delete it.
- **Access your data.** Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
- **Correction.** You can ask us to correct your name or contact details. Once a round closes, the synthesis for that round has already been produced, so earlier responses cannot be retroactively rewritten.

For any privacy request, email harmony@greensoftware.foundation.

## Data storage and access

- **Personal information (name and email):** held in a secure store, accessible only to GSF staff and the Assembly facilitator.
- **Response content:** held as part of the Assembly record, separated from your identity for processing.
- **Processing infrastructure:** hosted in the EU where possible.
- **Who has access:** GSF staff and the facilitator can see your name, email and responses in our systems. The AI sees only content separated from identity. Other participants never see your individual responses; they see only synthesised candidate positions. The public sees only agreed outputs and anonymised synthesis.
- **Third parties:** we use Anthropic (AI synthesis), Google (email) and GitHub (public discussion and the eventual open-source conventions). These involve data transfers to the US under appropriate safeguards.

## Retention

We keep your name and email for the duration of the Assembly and for up to 24 months afterwards, for follow-up about GSF programmes, unless you ask us to delete them sooner. Response content and the cryptographic audit record are retained as part of the Assembly's record. We may use anonymised synthesis and outcomes for future research, case studies or methodology improvements.

## Geographic considerations

Participants may join from anywhere. We have designed our handling to meet GDPR requirements for European participants:

- Processing occurs in the EU where possible.
- You have rights of access, deletion and portability.
- Our lawful basis is your consent, given by accepting the participation terms and taking part.
- Our use of third-party services (Anthropic, Google, GitHub) involves transfers to the US, and those providers have appropriate safeguards in place.
